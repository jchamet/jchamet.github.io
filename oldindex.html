About

I've undertaken a host of small projects and have worked for a handful of well-established companies of differing industries. As a result, I've worn many titles and taken turns as the researcher, the designer, the programmer, the marketer, the manager, and the lowly intern. As a result, I have a unique ability to partake in multifaceted businesses. And my personal life is just as diverse as my work life. When I'm not coding or pushing pixels, you'll find me deep in a good read, outside playing rugby, or carving ice.

designer

Art does not move like music, and it does not function like a program. It does not create but simply exists, a concrete totem glorifying a purpose or a cause. Visual displays are not always easy to create and often the work has to be redone over and over again, each time from scratch, before you finally succeed in painting on canvas the image in your head. But art is complete. And when you're finished you can step away and admire your work like a sculpter.

<programmer>

I love the logic and structure of programming. No project is truly beautiful if it doesn't have elegant and efficient code, whether it be HTML, CSS or jQuery, to match the quality of its interface. And for great functionality, SQL, PHP and AJAX knowledge is a must. Programming is more to me than just typing away. It isn't without emotion, without thought, or without difficulty. It is a careful passion, and where the designer is the architect, the programmer is the contractor, building the components that fit together.

♫musician

Music is great fun to listen to and to compose, but not so easy to perfect, and so it provides me with the perfect challenge: adding to the musical world. It is both understanding how sounds are created, and not understanding how they are so capable at affecting us that makes music friendly and familiar yet distant and mysterious. Music is calculated and free flowing, constrained yet shapeless, a truly fascinating art medium. I was introduced to music at a young age and took up the violin as my first instrument. Since then, I have tried others, including the electrical guitar and the mandolin.
